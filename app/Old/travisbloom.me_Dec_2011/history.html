A Brief History (excluding all that boring childhood stuff):

An ever-evolving timeline of my life from 2009 onward...

I chose to enroll at the University of Connecticut as a Honors student after graduating from Weston High School (where I served as President of the Senior Class). While the in-state tuition was definately a motivating factor, the UConn Business School's reputation was the main attraction to the campus in Storrs, CT.

The amazing basketball program helped make the decision a little easier as well (the picture shows the student moshpit after we became National Champs in 2011).

Originally my major was Management with a focus in entrepreneurship; I thought my ability to lead and delegate made the decision obvious. It wasn't until the end of my freshman year that I realized Management Information Systems aligned perfectly with my passion for technology. My love of startups, gadgets, mobile, social, and everything in between made the switch too attractive to pass up.

From the start of my freshman year I wanted to get involved in campus life. I served as a Senator in the Undergraduate Student Government, representing first year honors students like myself. This was an engaging role, but after pledging the Upsilon Kappa Chapter of my fraternity, Alpha Epsilon Pi, I knew Greek Life was where I wanted to devote my time.

At the beginning of my Sophomore year, I gained two important leadership roles within Greek Life. In my own chapter I was elected Secretary and on the community level I was elected to be the Interfraternity Council's VP of Financial Affairs.

Detailed descriptions of both roles can be found on my resume.

During the Summer of my Sophomore year I was fortunate enough to have my first real internship. As a Travel Retail Marketing intern at Estee Lauder's global headquarters in New York City, I spent 10 weeks performing various projects to benefit my employer. The highlight of my experience was a digital presentation I created and delivered. It was so well recieved that I was tasked with planning and executing the first mobile initative within the department.

After a year of performing my duties as Secretary, I was elected to be President of my fraternity. My term has just started and I remain excited and optimistic about the next year at UConn.

The first few months of my Junior year were somewhat stressful as I prepared and submitted applications for various IT internships. My number one choice was General Electric's prestigious Information Management Leadership Program. After applying being interviewed, I was offered a position for the Summer of 2012. I eagerly await the opportunity to work for a company globally known for investing in its employees and look forward to the various challenges it will offer.
